# 59420 Engineering Physics

## First Year

### 113 (8) Physics for Engineering students (2L, 0.5P, 0.5T)

Introduction to physics and physical quantities, including introduction to atomic physics; oscillatory motion,
introduction to wave motion, superposition and standing waves, sound waves, light, refraction, polarisation;
introduction to nuclear physics.

### 152 (6) Physics for Engineering students (2L, 1T)

Introduction to basic relativity and basic quantum mechanics. Continued study of waves, acoustics and
optics based on Engineering Physics 113.

**Prerequisite modules:**
- Engineering Physics 113



# **7. Postgraduate module contents of specific** **postgraduate degree programmes**

## **Centre for Bioinformatics and Computational Biology**

**BScHons in Bioinformatics and Computational Biology**

**14241 – 711 (10)** **Scientific Computing in Bioinformatics**

Introduction to Linux, programming in Python, variables and data structures, conditional execution, program
flow, reading and writing from/to disc, functions, exception handling, classes and object-oriented code,
regular expressions, using Python libraries.

**14242 – 712 (10)** **Cell Biology in Bioinformatics**

Origin of life and evolution, cell biology, cell respiration, inheritance of genetic material and gene expression.

**14234 – 713 (5)** **Current Topics in Bioinformatics**

Current and classic research publications are presented and discussed in a group context.

**14235 – 714 (40)** **Advanced Bioinformatics**

Introduction to biostatistics, databases, next-generation sequencing technologies, genomics and functional
genomics, sequence analysis, biological databases and ontologies, evolution and phylogenetics, population
genetics and GWAS, structural bioinformatics, proteomics, metabolomics, networks and pathways, and HPC

use.

**14236 – 715 (5)** **Bioinformatics Seminar**

A seminar is prepared and presented in which a project is proposed, including a review of the relevant
literature, identification of the aim, and presentation of a feasible workplan to achieve the aim.

**14237 – 716 (5)** **Algorithms in Bioinformatics**

Algorithms with special applications in bioinformatics will be reviewed and the applications thereof studied.
This will include algorithms used in sequence-similarity searches, suffix tree construction, genome
alignment, database searches, phylogenetic reconstructions, genome rearrangements, motif finding and
peptide sequencing.

**14238 – 717 (5)** **Machine Learning in Bioinformatics**

Linear and logistic regression, regularization, neural networks, system design, support vector machines,
unsupervised learning, dimensionality reduction, applications.

**14240 – 721 (50)** **Project in Bioinformatics**

A research project performed under supervision.

**MSc in Bioinformatics of Infectious Diseases and Pathogen Genomics**

**12555 – 811 (35)** **Bioinformatics**

The following topics will be reviewed: concepts in statistics for bioinformatics, next-generation sequencing
technologies, introduction to Linux and high-performance computing, genomics, functional genomics,
sequence analysis, biological databases and ontologies, evolution and phylogenetics, population genetics,
structural bioinformatics, proteomics, and microbiome analysis.

**71030 – 845 (25)** **Bioinformatics of Infectious Diseases**

Tuberculosis (TB) host genetics, genetic epidemiology and population genetics of TB susceptibility, extreme
phenotypes of TB susceptibility and resistance, TB pharmacogenetics, immunology of TB, TB diagnostics,
animal models in TB, introduction to mycobacteriology, the _Mycobacterium tuberculosis_ genome and
transcriptome, molecular tools for mycobacteriology, mycobacterial secretion systems, molecular diagnosis,
TB drugs, drug resistance mechanisms, mycobacterial metabolism, _M. tuberculosis_ in the macrophage, TB
latency/persisters, veterinary TB, TB microbiome.

**71031 – 846 (25)** **Pathogen Genomics**

Model pathogens, including viruses, bacteria, archaea, fungi, and eukaryotes, pathogen genomes, proteins
and functions, genome replication, technologies for sequencing pathogen genomes, Sanger and nextgeneration sequencing, genome assembly, mutational analysis, evolution, and phylogenetics.

**71025 – 813 (10)** **Python for Bioinformatics**

Brief introduction to and overview of the Python programming language applied to topics in bioinformatics.
